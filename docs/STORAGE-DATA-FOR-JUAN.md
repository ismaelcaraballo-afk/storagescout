# SETUP: The Battery Revolution

## Battery Costs Dropped 93%

| Year | Cost per kWh | How Much Cheaper Than 2010 |
|------|-------------|---------------------------|
| 2010 | $2,571 | -- (starting point) |
| 2015 | ~$800 | 69% cheaper |
| 2020 | ~$400 | 84% cheaper |
| 2023 | $310 | 88% cheaper |
| 2024 | $192 | 93% cheaper |

Source: IRENA, Renewable Power Generation Costs 2024

**Translation:** In 2010, storing enough energy to power about 30 homes for an hour cost
$2,571. Today that same storage costs $192. The technology got cheaper faster than almost
anything in energy history.

**Why:** A cheaper battery chemistry called LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) took over the
market. It now makes up 85% of all new grid batteries. LFP is cheaper, safer, and lasts
longer than the old chemistry (NMC).

---

## US Battery Storage Is Growing Fast

| Year | Total US Battery Storage | New That Year |
|------|-------------------------|---------------|
| 2020 | 1.5 GW | ~0.5 GW |
| 2022 | 8.8 GW | 4.2 GW |
| 2024 | 26 GW | 10.4 GW |
| 2025 | 45+ GW (projected) | 19.6 GW |
| 2026 | 65 GW (projected) | ~20 GW |

Source: EIA (Energy Information Administration)

**What is a GW?** A gigawatt = 1 billion watts. 1 GW can power roughly 750,000 homes.
So 26 GW could theoretically power about 19.5 million homes -- but only for a few hours,
because current batteries store 2-4 hours of energy.

**Key stat:** Battery + solar = 81% of ALL new US power capacity in 2025. These are not
side projects anymore. They are the main event.

# CONFLICT: The System Is Broken

## 2,300 GW Is Stuck Waiting to Connect

| Stat | Number |
|------|--------|
| Total projects waiting for grid connection | ~10,300 |
| Total capacity waiting | 2,300 GW |
| How that compares to what we have now | 2x the entire US power grid |
| Storage specifically waiting | 890 GW |
| How long the average project waits | 4+ years |
| Projects that actually get built | 13 out of 100 |
| Projects that gave up | 77 out of 100 |

Source: Berkeley Lab, 'Queued Up: 2025 Edition'

**What is the queue?** Before a battery or solar project can send power to the grid, it
needs approval from the grid operator. That process used to take about 2 years. Now it
takes over 4. The paperwork, studies, and transmission upgrades create a massive bottleneck.

**The key number:** Only 13% of projects that applied between 2000-2019 ever got built.
77% withdrew -- they gave up waiting.

---

## California Is Wasting Clean Energy

| Year | Energy Thrown Away (CAISO) |
|------|--------------------------|
| 2022 | ~1.5 million MWh |
| 2023 | 2.6 million MWh |
| 2024 | 3.4 million MWh |

Source: EIA

**What is happening:** California generates so much solar power at midday that the grid
cannot absorb it all. Without enough batteries to store the surplus, they literally have
to throw it away. 93% of the wasted energy was solar.

**This is called the 'duck curve'** -- when you graph the power demand minus solar generation,
the shape looks like a duck. The belly of the duck (midday) keeps getting deeper as more
solar comes online. Batteries fill that belly by charging at noon and discharging at sunset.

## Current Batteries Only Last 4 Hours

| Duration | Technology | Where It Stands | Target Cost |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| 0-4 hours | Lithium-ion (LFP) | This is what we have now | $192/kWh |
| 8-24 hours | Iron-sodium, zinc, flow batteries | Being tested | $150-300/kWh |
| 100+ hours | Iron-air (Form Energy) | First factory just opened | Target: <$20/kWh |

**The issue:** Almost all grid batteries today are 4-hour lithium-ion. That handles the daily
peak (sunset to late evening). But what about a week-long heat wave? A winter storm that
lasts days? 4 hours is not enough for real grid reliability.

**Form Energy** is the most interesting solution right now:
- Their battery uses iron and air -- literally rusting and un-rusting metal plates
- Stores energy for 100+ hours (vs 4 for lithium-ion)
- Target cost: under $20/kWh (vs $192 for lithium-ion)
- Google signed a deal for a 300 MW / 30 GWh system in Minnesota
- First factory is open in West Virginia

Source: Form Energy, Google/Xcel Energy

## The Money Problem

| Stat | Number |
|------|--------|
| Investment needed by 2030 | $1.4 trillion |
| That equals | The last 12 years of spending, compressed into 5 |
| Battery projects at risk of losing tax credits | 83% |
| Debt financing dropped in 2024 | 30% |

Source: Deloitte 2025 Renewable Energy Outlook

**Tax credits:** The government offers a 30-50% tax credit for building battery storage.
But new laws (One Big Beautiful Bill Act, July 2025) are phasing these out and adding
requirements that batteries must be made with US-manufactured parts. Problem: China makes
98% of the key battery component (LFP cathode material). US factories are being built but
most are not running yet.

---

## China Controls the Supply Chain

| What | China Share |
|------|------------|
| All battery manufacturing | 80%+ |
| LFP battery cells | 94-98% |
| LFP cathode material | 98%+ |
| Global clean energy investment | $800B (US is ~$300B) |

Source: IEA, BloombergNEF

**Why this matters:** The US wants to build its own battery supply chain (380 new factories
announced since the IRA). But right now, almost every grid battery installed in America
has Chinese-made cells inside it. That is a strategic risk -- if trade tensions escalate,
the supply could be disrupted.

## Battery Revenue Is Crashing

| Year | Avg Revenue per kWh (Texas) |
|------|-----------------------------|
| 2023 | $149/kWh |
| 2025 | $17/kWh |
| Change | -89% |

Source: Modo Energy

**What happened:** When Texas had only a few batteries, they made huge money from 'ancillary
services' (getting paid to keep the grid stable). Now that thousands of batteries are online,
that market is saturated. Revenue dropped 89% in two years.

**Why it matters for our pitch:** This is the opportunity. Battery owners need smart software
to maximize revenue. The hardware is cheap -- the value is in the optimization.

## Safety (Be Honest About This)

| Stat | Number |
|------|--------|
| Batteries that had a fire in 2024 | 0.3% of all projects |
| Improvement in failure rate since 2018 | 98% fewer failures |
| Grid batteries deployed since 2018 | 20x more |
| Fire frequency | Not increasing despite way more batteries |

**Big incidents:** Moss Landing, CA (Jan 2025) -- fire at the world's largest battery site.
1,200 people evacuated. But it used older NMC chemistry, not the newer safer LFP.

**Bottom line:** Fires are rare and getting rarer. The shift to LFP makes them even less
likely. But we should mention this before someone else brings it up.

Source: EPRI
