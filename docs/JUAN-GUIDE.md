## SECTION 3 — You Are Talking

This is YOUR section. Ismael is on the laptop. You have 60 seconds.

The screen shows the **US Battery Storage Capacity bar chart** (blue bars, 2019-2024).

Here is your script. Read it out loud a few times before Saturday. You don't have to memorize it word-for-word, but hit every number:

> "Look at this chart. 2019 — 1.2 gigawatts. 2020 — 1.8. Then the IRA hits in 2022 and watch what happens. 2022 — 8.9. 2023 — 16.2. 2024 — 26 gigawatts. That's a 490% increase in three years. Policy was the ignition switch."

Then pause and say:

> "But now scroll down one chart."

Ismael will scroll to **The Gap** dual-axis chart. It has a blue line (storage going up) and a red line (emissions barely moving).

Then continue:

> "This is the conflict. StorageScout calls it 'The Gap.' The blue line is battery storage — shooting up. The red line is power sector CO2 emissions — barely moving. 1,724 million metric tons in 2019, down to 1,546 in 2024. That's only a 10% drop while storage grew 2,067%."

Then explain why:

> "Why the gap? Two reasons. First — 2,300 gigawatts of projects are stuck in the interconnection queue. Average wait: 5 years. Second — the global race. China invested $800 billion in clean energy in 2025 — 3.5 times more than the US. We're in the gap years."

Then wrap up:

> "That's what this chart shows. The growth is real. The gap is also real."

Ismael scrolls to the next section. You're done talking.
